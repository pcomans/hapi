Recurring issues encountered when reviewing Porter & Moss I.2 (Royal Tombs) chunks for the porter-moss-theban-necropolis source.

**Why:** PM I.2 was published in 1964 and its attributions reflect the Egyptological consensus of that decade. Modern scholarship has revised several headword attributions (e.g. KV5 = sons of Ramesses II, not Ramesses II himself; KV9 doorways usurped from Ramesses V, the unnamed-in-headword predecessor). Extracts that mirror PM verbatim are *bibliographically* correct but can mislead downstream users. The scope decision (PM-as-source vs. modern-consensus-as-source) needs to be locked at the schema level.

**How to apply:**
1. PM-verbatim is the documented stance for `occupant_name` and `dynasty` (per README "occupant_name ... drawn verbatim from PM's headword"). Flag where this conflicts with modern attribution but accept the row if PM's text supports it. The fix is a downstream `attribution_status` or `modern_revision` field, not a row edit.
2. `is_unfinished` is defined as "literally contains the word Unfinished" in PM's headword. KV3 (Ramesses III's *original* tomb, abandoned and reused for Amenherkhepshef as KV13's neighbour, but PM's flag stays true) and KV5 (PM 1964 marked unfinished; Weeks 1995+ showed it's a finished and extensively used family complex — directly contradicts PM but the field's PM-verbatim semantics are correct).
3. `notes_from_pm` fragments are extracted as short verbatim prose. Watch for fragments that lose their grammatical referent (e.g. KV9 "doorways in outer part usurped from" — the *agent* of usurpation is named in the next clause that was dropped). Either expand to a complete clause or drop entirely; truncated mid-sentence quotes are useless to downstream users.
4. BCE dates in this source follow Kitchen high chronology (Ramesses II -1279 to -1213). HKW 2006 mid-chronology gives -1279 to -1213 also for R.II — so for Dyn 19/20 Ramessides the gap is small (±5 years). For earlier dynasties (12, 13, 18 early) the divergence is larger; flag any chunk that uses a different chronology than HKW.
5. `dynasty` is normalised to Arabic-numeral string. PM prints Roman numerals in section headers but not in headword lines (PM doesn't state dynasty in the headword for KV1-10). The Arabic-string form is consistent with how `pharaoh-se` and `hkw-chronology-2006` already encode dynasty. Don't switch to Roman.
6. `shared_with_tombs` cross-refs to chunks not yet landed (e.g. KV11 from chunk 1 → KV11 in chunk 2) are dangling pointers. Acceptable so long as a structural test verifies they resolve once the later chunk lands. Confirm such a test exists or recommend adding one.
7. KV5's PM headword `(L. D. Text and WILKINSON, No. 5, BURTON, M.)` does NOT include the (now-canonical) attribution to "Sons of Ramesses II". PM 1964 simply lists it under Ramesses II. If a future chunk extends to KV5 enrichment (post-Weeks rediscovery), the source would be a Weeks/TMP authority, not PM I.2.
8. **PM-verbatim spelling must survive the pipeline even for unusual forms.** Chunk-2 KV19 shipped with `"Ramesses-Mentuherkhepshef"` (modern-consensus form) despite PM p. 546 printing `"Ra'meses-Mentuhirkhopshef"` (apostrophe + `hir` not `her` + `khopshef` not `khepshef`). The extraction agents silently normalised to the familiar modern form. The first-pass egyptologist reviewer also missed it because the modern form "looks right". KV15/KV17 correctly preserve PM's `Sethos` — so the rule is understood, just applied inconsistently for the less-famous princes. **How to catch:** always re-open the PDF at the cited page and compare the headword *glyph-for-glyph* against `occupant_name`, especially for non-royal / rarely-cited individuals where no modern spelling sanity-check triggers.
9. **PM headword role qualifiers must land in `notes_from_pm`.** KV13 PM prints "BAY, Chancellor. Temp. Merneptah-Siptah." First-pass fixed notes to `"Temp. Merneptah-Siptah"` but dropped "Chancellor" entirely, even though the controlled-vocab `occupant_role` was coarsened to `"Official"`. When role gets coarsened for controlled-vocab compliance, the specific PM title (Chancellor / Vizier / High Priest / Overseer-of-X) must be preserved in `notes_from_pm` — otherwise the data layer loses information that's present in the source. Same pattern as chunk-1 issue 3 (truncated notes).
10. **OCR confuses `ḥ` (h-with-dot-below in PM small-caps) with `i`.** Chunk-3 KV36 shipped `Maihirper`; PM p. 556 prints `MAḤIRPER`. The extraction agent (or OCR upstream) rendered `Ḥ` → `i` — a real shape confusion in scanned small caps where the dot disappears and the descenderless `H` silhouette resembles a narrow `I`. Same failure mode as chunk-2 KV19 issue 8 (silent normalisation of unfamiliar non-royal names), but the mechanism here is OCR not modernisation. **How to catch:** for any non-royal occupant, re-open the PDF headword and check every `h`-adjacent vowel for a missing dot-below. If the name has no modern form to sanity-check against, this is the only way.
11. **Diacritic-stripping policy is inconsistent across chunks.** KV19 shipped `Raʿmeses-Mentuhirkhopshef` preserving the apostrophe/ayin. KV45 ships `Userhet` stripping the ḥ and ē PM printed as `USERḤĒT`. KV46 notes strip circumflexes from `harîm`/`Amûn`. The project needs a documented policy (ASCII-only? preserve all Unicode? preserve only ayin but drop macrons?). Flag the inconsistency in review; don't silently pick one. Suggested resolution: README or source-level `diacritic_policy` field, enforced by a structural test.
