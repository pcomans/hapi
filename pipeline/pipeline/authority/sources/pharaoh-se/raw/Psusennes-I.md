# Psusennes I in hieroglyphs

The third pharaoh of the [Twenty-first Dynasty](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/dynasty/21),

| Precedessor<br>[Amenemnesut](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/pharaoh/Amenemnesut)<br>Successor<br>[Amenemope](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/pharaoh/Amenemope) |
| Reign of **Psusennes I** |
| AE Chronology | 1051–1006 |
| von Beckerath | 1044–994 |
| Shaw | 1039–991 |
| Dodson | 1034–981 |
| Redford | 1055–1004 |
| Manetho (Africanus) | 46 years |
| Manetho (Eusebius) | 41 years |
| Manetho (Jerome) | 41 years |

Psusennes is the ancient Greek transcription of his original name Pasibkhanu or Pasebakhaenniut. His tomb in Tanis was discovered completely intact in 1940, but the damp climate of Lower Egypt had destroyed almost everything but metal objects.

## Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/21-03-01.svg)

Ka nakht em ded Amun, user fau,
sekha em Waset

The strong bull through the gift of Amun, rich in splendor, who has been made to appear in Thebes

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)
