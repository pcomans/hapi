## The Royal Titulary

From the hieroglyphic records

## Throne name

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

Throne name

![Hedj kheper Ra](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/22-08-01.svg)

Hedj kheper Ra

ḥḏ ḫpr rꜤ

The bright one is a manifestation of Ra

ra-S1-xpr-stp&n&ra

Montet, _Les Constructions et le tombeau de Chéchanq III a Tanis_ (1960), 76, pl. 49

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 190-191, 7:T

## Birth names

![Nomen or birth name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_sara.svg)

Personal name given at birth. Also known as the _Nomen_.

Birth name

![Shesheneq <i>netjer heqa Iunu</i>](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/22-08-02.svg)

Shesheneq _netjer heqa Iunu_

ššnḳ nṯr ḥḳꜢ iwnw

Shoshenq, the god and ruler of Heliopolis

M8A-M8A-n:q-G7-nTr-HqA-iwn

Year 10 stela (St. Petersburg Hermitage 5630)

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 190-191, 7:E1

Birth name variant

![Shesheneq <i>mery Amun sa Bastet netjer heqa Iunu</i>](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/22-08-03.svg)

Shesheneq _mery Amun sa Bastet netjer heqa Iunu_

ššnḳ mry imn sꜢ bꜢstt nṯr ḥḳꜢ iwnw

Shoshenq, beloved of Amun, son of Bastet, the god and ruler of Heliopolis
