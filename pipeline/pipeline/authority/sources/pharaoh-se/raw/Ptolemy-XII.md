## Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

## Nebty names

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nebty.svg)

The “Two Ladies”, the _goddesses Nekhbet and Wadjet_, representing Upper and Lower Egypt respectively.

Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/33-12-02.svg)

Wer pehty khenty she neheh semen hepu mi Djehuty aa

wr-pḥtꞽ ḫntš-nḥḥ smn-hpw-mꞽ-ḏḥwtꞽ-ꜤꜢ-ꜤꜢ

The one great of strength and foremost one of the sea forever, who has established laws like the twice-great Thoth

wr:F9\*F9:Z9\*t-N37A:N36:H\*ra\*H-s-U28-O4A\*p:Z2:H27-mi-G191-O29v-O29v

## Golden Horus names

![Golden Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_gold.svg)

Horus perched on Gold, which was associated with eternity, but its meaning is disputed.

Golden Horus name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/33-12-04.svg)

ꜤꜢ-ꞽb ꞽty nb-ḳnw-nḫt-mꞽ-zꜢ-Ꜣst

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

![Iwa en pa netjer nehem setep en Ptah ir Maat en Ra Sekhem ankh Imen](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/33-12-06.svg)

The heir of the saviour god, who is the chosen of Ptah, who brings forth the order of Ra, the living image of Amun
