# Beon in hieroglyphs

A pharaoh(?) of the [Fifteenth Dynasty](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/dynasty/15), _a.k.a. Bnon_

| Precedessor<br>[Sharek](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/pharaoh/Sharek)<br>Successor<br>[Djehuty](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/pharaoh/Djehuty) |
| Reign of **Beon** |

Several attempts has been made to identify _Beon_ or _Bnon_ with an archaeologically attested ruler, but his hieroglyphic name(s) remains unidentified, and the name Βηών/Βνῶν can not be linked to an actual royal name.

The Greek letter β is also used in Greek for the number 2, written as β′. The original note in Manetho may have shown that the second king (i.e. β′) was “without name” (i.e. β′ \+ νώνυμος, or _2 + nonymos_, ), indicating that the king's name was unknown. Perhaps a copy of the Epitome was made when the shorthand νών had become the norm, or the copyist made a careless dittographical error and corrupted the text into Βνῶν instead of β′ \+ νώνυμος?1
