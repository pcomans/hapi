## Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/23-05-01.svg)

Ka nakht kha em Waset

kꜢ-nḫt ḫꜤꞽ-m.wꜢst

The strong bull has appearaed in Thebes

E1:D40-xa-m-S40

Legrain, _Le temple et les chapelles d'Osiris a Karnak_, RecTrav 22 (1900): 129

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 194-195, 5:H

## Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nebty.svg)

The “Two Ladies”, the _goddesses Nekhbet and Wadjet_, representing Upper and Lower Egypt respectively.

Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/23-05-02.svg)

Set ib tawy

st-ꞽb-tꜢwꞽ

The favorite one of the Two Lands

st-ib:N17:N17

Legrain, _Le temple et les chapelles d'Osiris a Karnak_, RecTrav 22 (1900): 129

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 194-195, 5:N

## Golden Horus name

![Golden Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_gold.svg)

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.
