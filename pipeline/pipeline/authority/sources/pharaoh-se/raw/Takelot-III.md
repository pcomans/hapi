## Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/23-06-01.svg)

Wadj tawy

wꜢḏ-tꜢwꞽ

The sturdy one of the Two Lands

M14-N17:N17

Legrain, _Le temple et les chapelles d'Osiris a Karnak_, RecTrav 22 (1900): 128

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 194-195, 6:H

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

Throne name

![User Maat Ra, <i>setep en Amun</i>](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/23-06-04.svg)

User Maat Ra, _setep en Amun_

wsr-mꜢꜤt-rꜤ stp.n-ꞽmn

The strong one belonging to the Maat of Ra, chosen by Amun

ra-wsr-mAat-i-mn:n:stp

Daressy, _Inscriptions inédites de la XXIe Dynastie_, RecTrav **18** (1896): 51

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 194-195, 6:T1
