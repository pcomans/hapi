# Harsiese A in hieroglyphs

The second pharaoh of the [Unplaced kings](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/dynasty/unplaced),

| Precedessor<br>[Anather](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/pharaoh/Anather)<br>Successor<br>[Ini II](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/pharaoh/Ini-II) |
| Reign of **Harsiese A** |
|  | 880–860 |
| Dodson | 867–857 |

Independent king that ruled for several years at Thebes during Takelot I's and Osorkon II's reigns.

## Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/36-01-01.svg)

Ka nakht kha em Waset

kꜢ-nḫt ḫꜤꞽ-m-wꜢst

The strong bull who appeared in Thebes

E2:D40-m&xa-R19

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

Throne name

![Hedj kheper Ra, <i>setep en Amun</i>](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/36-01-02.svg)

Hedj kheper Ra, _setep en Amun_

ḥḏ-ḫpr-rꜤ stp.n-ꞽmn

The radiant one is a manifestation of Ra, chosen by Amun

ra-S1-xpr-i-mn:n-stp:n

Sarcophagus of Harsiese (Cairo Museum JE 59896)

Legrain, _Note sur deux monuments provenant de Kouft_, ASAE **6** (1905): 123

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 196-197, b:T
