Khafra was a son of Khufu and succeeded his brother Radjedef. It is possible that his name actually was **Rakhaf**, using the same pattern as his brother, with Ra at the beginning of the name rather than at the end as is the norm used in Egyptology. Rakhaf translates to _"Ra is his (very) appearance"_, which actually makes sense, so it is not unreasonable to consider it. Herodotus and Diodorus transcribed his name as **Khephren**, which suggests that Khafra would fit as the source. Manetho named him _Souphis_.

He is of course known as the builder of the second largest pyramid at Giza, but little else is known about him.
