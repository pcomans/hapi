## Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/13-08-01.svg)

Seheru tawy

shrw-tꜢwꞽ

The one who has pleased the Two Lands

s-O4A:r:N17:N17

Sandstone altar of Ameny (Cairo Museum CG 23040, JE 39804)

Mariette, _Karnak_, II, plates 9-10

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 90-91, 7:H

## Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nebty.svg)

The “Two Ladies”, the _goddesses Nekhbet and Wadjet_, representing Upper and Lower Egypt respectively.

Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/13-08-02.svg)

Sekhem khau

sḫm-ḫꜤw

Powerful of appearances

s-sxm-xa:a-w

Sandstone altar of Ameny (Cairo Museum CG 23040, JE 39804)

Mariette, _Karnak_, II, plates 9-10

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 90-91, 7:N

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

Throne name

![Sankh ib Ra](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/13-08-05.svg)

Sankh ib Ra

sꜤnḫ-ꞽb-rꜤ

The one whom the mind of Ra has sustained

ra-s-anx-ib

Sandstone altar of Ameny (Cairo Museum CG 23040, JE 39804)

Mariette, _Karnak_, II, plates 9-10

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 90-91, 7:T1

Throne name variant

![Sankh ib Ra](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/13-08-06.svg)

Sankh ib Ra

sꜤnḫ-ꞽb-rꜤ

The one whom the mind of Ra has sustained

ra-s-anx-n:x-ib-1

Turin King List 7.10

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 90-91, 7:T2

Gardiner, _The Royal Canon of Turin_ (1959) VI:10
