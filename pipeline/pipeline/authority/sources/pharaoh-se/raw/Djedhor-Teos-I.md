# Djedhor Teos I in hieroglyphs

The second pharaoh of the [Thirtieth Dynasty](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/dynasty/30), _a.k.a. Tachos, Tachus_

| Precedessor<br>[Nectanebo I](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/pharaoh/Nectanebo-I)<br>Successor<br>[Nectanebo II](https://pharaoh.se/ancient-egypt/pharaoh/Nectanebo-II) |
| Reign of **Djedhor Teos I** |
| AE Chronology | 365–360 |
| von Beckerath | 362–360 |
| Shaw | 362–360 |
| Dodson | 365–360 |
| Redford | 362–361 |
| Manetho | 2 years |

Teos was betrayed by his own brother while on an expedition abroad. His brother put his own son on the throne and Teos was forced to flee to the court of the Persians. He was ultimately brought back to Egypt in chains and to a, most likely, bitter end.

## Horus names

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

## Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nebty.svg)

The “Two Ladies”, the _goddesses Nekhbet and Wadjet_, representing Upper and Lower Egypt respectively.

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.
