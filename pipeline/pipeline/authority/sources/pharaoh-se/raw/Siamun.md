## Horus names

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/21-06-01.svg)

Ka nakht mery Maat, sa mery en Amun, per em hau.ef

kꜢ-nḫt mrꞽ-mꜢꜤt sꜢ mrꞽ n ꞽmn pr m ḫꜤw.f

The strong bull, beloved of Maat, the beloved son of Amun, who issued from his limbs

E1:D40-C10A-mr-H8:Z1-//-S3-i-mn:n-pr:r:D54-m-H-a:F51B\*F51B\*F51B:f

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

Throne name

![Netjeri kheper Ra, <i>setep en Amun</i>](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/21-06-03.svg)

Netjeri kheper Ra, _setep en Amun_

nṯrꞽ-ḫpr-rꜤ stp.n-ꞽmn

The divine one is a manifestation of Ra, chosen by Amun

ra-nTr-xpr-i-mn:n-U19:n

Petrie, _The palace of Apries (Memphis II)_, plate 24

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 180-181, 6:T1

Throne name variant

![Netjeri kheper Ra, <i>mery Amun</i>](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/21-06-06.svg)

Netjeri kheper Ra, _mery Amun_

nṯrꞽ-ḫpr-rꜤ mrꞽ-ꞽmn

The divine one is a manifestation of Ra, beloved of Amun

ra-nTr-xpr-i-mn:n:U7

Munier, _Un achat de terrains au temps du roi Si-amon_, RecChamp (1923): 361–6

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 180-181, 6:T2
