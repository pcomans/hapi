## Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/16-15-01.svg)

Wehem ankh

wḥm-Ꜥnḫ

The one who has repeated life

F25-anx

Colossal statue of Senusret IV (Cairo Museum, CG 42026, JE 36128)

Legrain, _Sur une stèle de Senousrit IV_, RecTrav **30** (1908): 15-16

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 102-103, a:H

## Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nebty.svg)

The “Two Ladies”, the _goddesses Nekhbet and Wadjet_, representing Upper and Lower Egypt respectively.

Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/16-15-02.svg)

Sankh tawy

sꜤnḫ-tꜢwꞽ

The one who has sustained the Two Lands

s-anx-N17:N17:N21\*N21

Colossal statue of Senusret IV (Cairo Museum, CG 42026, JE 36128)

Legrain, _Sur une stèle de Senousrit IV_, RecTrav **30** (1908): 15-16

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 102-103, a:N

## Golden Horus name

![Golden Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_gold.svg)

Nefer khau

nfr-xa:Z2

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

Throne name

![Senefer ib Ra](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/16-15-04.svg)

Senefer ib Ra

snfr-ꞽb-rꜤ

The one whom Ra’s heart has made perfect

ra-s-nfr-ib

Colossal statue of Senusret IV (Cairo Museum, CG 42026, JE 36128)

Legrain, _Sur une stèle de Senousrit IV_, RecTrav **30** (1908): 15-16

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 102-103, a:T
