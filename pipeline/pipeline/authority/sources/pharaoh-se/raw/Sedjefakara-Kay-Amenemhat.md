## Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/13-19-01.svg)

Hery tep tawy

ḥrꞽ-tp-tꜢwi

The chieftain of the Two Lands

D2\*D1:N17:N17

Revue de l'Égypte ancienne, II, 159

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 92-93, 15:H

## Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nebty.svg)

The “Two Ladies”, the _goddesses Nekhbet and Wadjet_, representing Upper and Lower Egypt respectively.

Nebty name

![Nebty name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/13-19-02.svg)

Netjeri bau

nṯrꞽ-bꜢw

Divine of might

nTr-G30

Revue de l'Égypte ancienne, II, 159

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 92-93, 15:N

## Golden Horus name

![Golden Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_gold.svg)

Horus perched on Gold, which was associated with eternity, but its meaning is disputed.

Golden Horus name

Aa pehty

ꜤꜢ-pḥtꞽ

aA:F9\*F9

Revue de l'Égypte ancienne, II, 159

## Throne names

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

Throne name

![Sedjefa ka Ra](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/13-19-04.svg)

Sedjefa ka Ra

sḏfꜢ-kꜢ-rꜤ

The one whom the ka of Ra has endowed

ra-s-D:f-A-kA

Revue de l'Égypte ancienne, II, 159

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 92-93, 15:T1
