## Horus names

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_horus.svg)

The oldest form of the name of a pharaoh, a falcon representing Horus is perched on the palace facade.

Horus name

![Horus name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/34-05-01.svg)

Tjemaa hui khasut, wer nakhtu Baqet, heqa heqau, setep en Nenu merur

ṯmꜢ-Ꜥ ḥwꞽ-ḫꜢswt wr-nḫw-bꜢḳt ḥḳꜢ-ḥḳꜢw stp-n-nnw-mrwr

The sturdy-armed one who struck the foreign lands, victorious for Egypt, ruler or rulers, chosen of Nun who loves him

d:U2\*1:a-A24-N25:Z2-wr:r-n:xt:x\*D54-D10-HqA-HqA-HqA-stp:n-nw\*nw\*nw:N1-N36:Z9\*r

Weill, _Parthénios fils de Paminis, prostatès d’Isis à Koptos_, ASAE 12 (1912): 15

## Throne name

![Prenomen or throne name](https://pharaoh.se/svg/hiero/t_nesut.svg)

Announced at the coronation and always written in a cartouche. The “official” name of the pharaoh. Also known as the _Prenomen_.

Throne name

![Heqa heqau setep en Ptah mery Aset](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/34-05-03.svg)

Heqa heqau setep en Ptah mery Aset

ḥḳꜢ-ḥḳꜢw stp-n-ptḥ mrꞽ-Ꜣst

Ruker of rulers, chosen by Ptah, beloved of Isis

HqA-HqA-Z3-stp:n-C159A\\-C102-N36

Lepsius, _Denkmäler aus Ägypten und Äthiopien_, IV, 79

Beckerath, “Handbuch der ägyptischen Königsnamen”, 2nd ed. _MÄS_ **49** (1999). 254-255, 5:T1

## Autokrator

The Greek epithet _Autokrator_ is the equivalent of the Latin epithet _Imperator_ (Commander).

It was always written in the cartouche.

Autokrator

![Autokrator Neron](https://pharaoh.se/svg/pharaoh/34-05-04.svg)

Autokrator Neron

Emperor Nero
